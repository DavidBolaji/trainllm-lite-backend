UK VISA FREQUENTLY ASKED QUESTIONS

This document provides general information about common UK visa categories, requirements, and processes. It is intended for informational purposes only.

1. Common UK Visa Types

The most common UK visa categories include:
- Skilled Worker Visa
- Student Visa
- Family Visa
- Visitor Visa
- Global Talent Visa

Each visa type has specific eligibility criteria and documentation requirements.

2. Skilled Worker Visa

The Skilled Worker visa allows foreign nationals to work in the UK for an approved employer.

Key requirements include:
- A valid Certificate of Sponsorship from a licensed UK employer
- A job offer that meets the minimum skill and salary threshold
- Proof of English language proficiency
- Sufficient funds to support yourself unless your sponsor covers this

The visa is usually granted for up to five years and can lead to indefinite leave to remain.

3. Student Visa

The UK Student visa is for individuals accepted into an approved educational institution.

Requirements include:
- Confirmation of Acceptance for Studies (CAS)
- Proof of funds to pay tuition and living costs
- English language ability
- Valid passport and travel history

Students may work limited hours during term time depending on their course level.

4. Family Visa

A Family visa allows individuals to join family members who are UK citizens or permanent residents.

Eligible relationships include:
- Spouse or partner
- Parent
- Child

Applicants must meet relationship, financial, accommodation, and English language requirements.

5. Visitor Visa

The Standard Visitor visa allows short-term visits for tourism, business, or family visits.

Key points:
- Usually valid for up to six months
- No paid or unpaid work allowed
- Proof of intent to return to home country is required

6. Processing Times and Fees

Processing times vary depending on visa type and country of application. Priority services may be available for faster decisions at an additional cost.

Visa fees are subject to change and depend on visa category and duration.

7. Important Notes

- Visa rules may change frequently.
- Meeting eligibility requirements does not guarantee approval.
- Applicants should rely on official UK government guidance for final decisions.
