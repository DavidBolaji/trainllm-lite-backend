CANADA IMMIGRATION OVERVIEW

This document provides an overview of common Canadian immigration pathways and requirements.

1. Permanent Residence Pathways

Canada offers several routes to permanent residence, including:
- Express Entry
- Provincial Nominee Program (PNP)
- Family Sponsorship
- Atlantic Immigration Program

Each pathway has specific eligibility criteria.

2. Express Entry System

Express Entry manages applications for three main programs:
- Federal Skilled Worker Program
- Federal Skilled Trades Program
- Canadian Experience Class

Candidates are ranked using the Comprehensive Ranking System (CRS), which considers:
- Age
- Education
- Work experience
- Language proficiency
- Adaptability factors

Invitations to Apply (ITAs) are issued periodically to top-ranking candidates.

3. Provincial Nominee Program

The Provincial Nominee Program allows provinces and territories to nominate individuals based on local labor market needs.

Key features:
- Nomination significantly increases CRS score
- Requirements vary by province
- Some streams require job offers, others do not

4. Family Sponsorship

Canadian citizens and permanent residents may sponsor eligible family members, including:
- Spouse or common-law partner
- Dependent children
- Parents and grandparents

Sponsors must meet income requirements and agree to provide financial support.

5. Study and Work Permits

Temporary residents may enter Canada using:
- Study permits for approved educational institutions
- Work permits, either employer-specific or open

Some temporary residents may transition to permanent residence through Canadian experience.

6. Processing Times

Processing times depend on application type, country of residence, and application completeness.

Applicants are encouraged to submit accurate and complete documentation to avoid delays.

7. Important Notes

- Immigration policies are subject to change.
- Program eligibility does not guarantee approval.
- Official Canadian government sources should be consulted for final guidance.
